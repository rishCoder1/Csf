Access to Technology

This section explores the availability and non-availability of smartphones, access to electricity, and internet at the household and individual/child level in India. Additionally, it captures data on possession of smartphones and shared access within households.

Availability of Electricity in Households

In both urban and rural households, more than 99% of respondents reported access to electricity.

Availability of Phones in Households

Households in Telangana reported the highest percentage of smartphone (97%). In five out of the six states, smartphone ownership was reported to be higher among urban households than rural households, with the exception of Uttar Pradesh (UP). In UP, 87% of rural households reported ownership of smartphones compared to 83% of urban households.

Availability of Internet on Smartphones

Overall, of the children who used smartphones, almost all children reported having access to the internet. However, around 50% of the children reported intermittent access to the internet (could access internet sometimes).

Extent of Smartphone Access Among Children

72% of the children from all the surveyed households had access to smartphones. Of them, 66% of the children had 'shared access' to smartphones, whereas 6% of children had 'dedicated access' and 29% did not have access.

Session Time on Smartphones

Overall, parents/guardians reported that 74% of children had more than 30 minutes of session time on smartphones on a given day. A higher proportion of children in rural households (30%) as compared to children in urban households (20%) spent more than 1 hour.
